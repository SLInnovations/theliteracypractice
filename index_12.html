Who This Is For

Does this sound familiar?

The child who dreads school

Reading and writing feel like the hardest things in the world. They're not struggling because they're not trying. Reading and spelling are skills. They can be taught — sometimes they just haven't been taught in the right way yet.

The child with a diagnosis

Dyslexia, ADHD, a processing difference. A label helps explain things, but what they really need is instruction built around how their brain works.

The child who can read but can't write

Reading clicked, but getting ideas onto paper is a different story. Writing and spelling are skills that can be taught, systematically, step by step.

The child who slipped through

School is busy. Gaps get missed. By the time a family finds me, those gaps have usually been growing quietly for a while. It's not too late to fill them.

The Approach

What I know to be true.

Literacy changes everything.

When a student can read and spell with confidence, the whole picture shifts. School gets easier. So does showing up for it.

Explicit instruction works.

Students who struggle need skills taught directly, systematically, and with lots of practice built in.

Every student has a roadmap.

I've never met a student who didn't want to be a better reader or writer. The job is finding their on-ramp and building from there.
